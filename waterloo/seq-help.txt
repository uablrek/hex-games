|Initial Deployment|
Welcome to the Napoleon at Waterloo game.
^
^If you want to zoom in/out, NOW is the time! Adjust your browser and
reload this page (F5) before you start the game.
^
^Enter - Start game

|test-mode|
t - Start game in Test Mode
^
^Test Mode:
^All units becomes draggable during movement phase, which let you
setup any situation. During Combat Resolution you will select the die roll.

|Reinforcements|
Reinforcements are available. Drag units from the UnitBox to a
rightmost hex on the map (23XX), out of forrest and enemy ZOC.
Reinforcment units may be stacked, but stacking rules must be obeyed
after the Movement phase. One movement factor (MF) is consumed for
entering the map.
^
Units dragged to an illegal hex will be returned to the UnitBox
before the Movement phase.
^
^Enter - Allied Movement phase
^Space - Rotate stack

|Movement|
Click on a friendly unit to select it and mark all possible
moves. Click on a marker to move the unit. Moved units have red
marks. Units may be temporary stacked during movement.
^
^Enter - Combat phase
^Esc - Un-select
^r - Regret move
^Space - Rotate stack

|Combat Assignment|
All engaged units have yellow marks and *must* fight.  Click on enemy
units to select them as a target for attack. They will be marked with
a cross-hair. then, select friendly units to attack the targets. The
odds is shown in the CRT. Unit assigned to combat will get green
marks. Repeat until all engaged units are assigned.
^
^Enter - Combat Resolution
^Esc - Re-do Combat Assignment

|Combat Resolution|
Select an engaged unit, they have green marks. The
odds is updated in the CRT.
^
^Enter - Commence Attack
^l - Voluntarily reduce odds
^Esc - Restore odds

|Roll the Die|
Test mode is enabled, and you must select the die roll.
^
^1-6 - Roll the Die

|Retreat|

Retreat after combat. Units that must retreat have a yellow mark.  If
a friendly unit is in the way it can be "displaced", unless it has
been in a battle. Retreat into enemy ZOC is not allowed. If a unit
can't retreat it must be deleted. The defender may choose to delete a
unit instead of retreating. Units that have not retreated will be
automatically deleted when moving to Advance phase.
^
^Enter - Advance phase
^d - Delete unit

|Remove Attackers|

Exhange (EX) combat result. All defenders are eliminated. The attacker
must remove combat factors equal or more than the defender losses.
^
^d - Delete unit

|Advance|
Advance after combat. Vacated hexes are marked, and units that can advance
have yellow marks. Click on a unit to select it, and on a vacated hex (if
there is more than one). Advancing is optional.
^^Advance *may* be done regardless of ZOC. Advance to a forrest hex is
only allowed via a road.
^^You can't "regret" an advance, so be careful
^
^Enter - Proceed without advancing

|overstacked|
There are overstacked counters. You can either remove units, or
regret/redo moves until the stacking rules are obeyed.

|combatrules|
All engaged units are not assigned to combat. Add attackers, or hit
'Esc' to reset the Combat Assignment phase

|Remove Attackers|
EX combat result. The attacker must remove combat factors (CF) equal
to the defenders losses, or more. Bombarding units are unaffected by
an EX result and may not be removed to fulfil the requirements

|remainingEX|
More attacker combat factors (CF) must be removed
